📚 Bookworm Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 7, 2026

Bookworm (@bookwormx402) is a service that lets X (Twitter) users gift Kindle eBooks to each other with a single tweet. This policy explains what data we collect, why, and how we use it.

1. What We Collect

  • X (Twitter) username — used solely to verify the identity of the gift recipient when they claim their book. We do not store your full Twitter profile or access your timeline.
  • Amazon account email address — provided voluntarily by the recipient to receive the gifted Kindle book. Used only to fulfill the gift order.
  • Payment information — handled entirely by Stripe. We never see or store your card details. See Stripe's Privacy Policy.
  • Public tweet content — we read public mentions of @bookwormx402 to process gift requests. We do not store your tweets beyond what is needed to fulfill the order.

2. How We Use Your Data

  • To verify the identity of a gift recipient via Twitter OAuth (read-only).
  • To deliver a gifted Kindle eBook to the recipient's Amazon account.
  • To process payments through Stripe.
  • We do not sell, rent, or share your data with third parties for marketing purposes.

3. Data Retention

Order data (usernames, Amazon email, Amazon link) is retained only as long as needed to fulfill the gift. Claim records are deleted after 30 days.

4. Third-Party Services

  • X (Twitter) — for reading public mentions and verifying recipient identity. Governed by X's Privacy Policy.
  • Stripe — for payment processing. Governed by Stripe's Privacy Policy.
  • Amazon — for Kindle gift delivery. Governed by Amazon's Privacy Notice.

5. Your Rights

You may request deletion of any data we hold about you by contacting us at support@bookworm402.com. We will respond within 30 days.

6. Contact

Questions? Reach us at support@bookworm402.com or @bookwormx402 on X.
